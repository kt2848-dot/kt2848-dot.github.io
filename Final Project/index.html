Overview

Japanese fingerstyle guitar is a solo acoustic approach that blends melody, harmony, bass lines, and percussive sounds into a single performance. Players often arrange full songs for one guitar, shaping dynamics and tone color with precise right-hand control. In this website, Page 1 introduces the concept and features representative artists with photos and short notes. Page 2 explains core techniques such as hammer-ons, pull-offs, and harmonics using anchored sections and supporting images or a demo video. Page 3 presents my own performance video as a portfolio-style showcase, plus brief performance notes.
