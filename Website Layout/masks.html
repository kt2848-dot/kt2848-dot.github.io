Typology

Noh masks are commonly grouped into families such as okina (ritual old man), onna (women), otoko (men), kishin (deities and demons), and specialized types like hannya or shikami. These lineages encode role, age, and liminality through proportion and surface finish.

Making

Masks are carved from hinoki cypress, finished with layered gofun (gesso) and urushi (lacquer). Depth at the mouth and eyes is carefully tuned to catch light, enabling expressive shifts with tiny rotations.

Workshop Measurements

Traditional templates specify interocular width, nose bridge contour, and mouth depth. Wear and repair patterns serve as a historical record of performance rather than flaws to be erased.
