Stage & Light

Actors rely on the shōmen viewpoint and high frontal lighting. Downward tilts darken the eye recesses and mouth (sorrow); upward tilts brighten the planes (joy). The mask’s carved geometry and finishing layers make these optical effects legible to the audience.

From Object to Presence

Through codified kata—precise patterns of motion—performers turn static wood into a living face. The result is less a disguise than a temporal bridge connecting present bodies with remembered roles and ritual time.
